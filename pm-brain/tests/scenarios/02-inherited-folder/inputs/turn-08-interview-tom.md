**Interviewer:** Maya Chen

## Why this call

Tom was in Alex's March interview log. He was the one who unprompted brought up shared budgets ("split rent and groceries with my girlfriend"). I wanted to follow up on whether that was a passing mention or a real ongoing need.

---

## Transcript (abridged)

**Maya:** Hey Tom. I saw in Alex's notes from March that you mentioned wanting to share a budget with your girlfriend. I wanted to ask how that's going two months later.

**Tom:** Oh man, it's still a thing. We've just been doing it in a shared Notes doc, which is terrible. We forget to update it, we argue about who paid what for groceries. I was actually about to try one of the couples-focused apps. There's one — Monarch? — that I think does this.

**Maya:** What stopped you from switching?

**Tom:** I like Tally. The streak counter, the simplicity. Monarch looks busy and expensive. But if Tally doesn't get shared budgets in the next few months I'll probably try Monarch anyway. We're at the point where the spreadsheet thing isn't working.

**Maya:** Help me understand what "shared budget" actually means to you. Same login? Separate logins viewing the same data?

**Tom:** Separate logins. Both see the same categories — rent, groceries, utilities. Both can add transactions. Maybe a flag for "joint" vs "mine." We don't want full visibility into each other's personal stuff. Just the joint pot.

**Maya:** Got it. Two more things. The spreadsheet export you mentioned in March — still want that?

**Tom:** Yeah, but lower priority than shared. If I had shared, I'd not need export.

**Maya:** And — AI insights? Anything like that come up for you in the last two months?

**Tom:** No. Honestly the only AI thing I want is for the category suggestions to be right more often.

- He explicitly rejected AI insights as something he wants. Adds to a small but accumulating "AI insights is a push, not a pull" pattern.
- Shared budgets hypothesis should probably move from candidate to **active** based on accumulating evidence — but per our rules, only if the agent agrees the threshold is met and the supporting evidence is independent. Note that the original CS monthly volume + Priya's sister + Tom's direct ask are three different observation types, not three iterations of one type.
