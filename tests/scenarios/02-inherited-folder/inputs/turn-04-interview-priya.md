# Customer interview — Priya M. (follow-up from Alex's Feb interview)

**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Interviewer:** Maya Chen (PM, new)
**Interviewee:** Priya M., Pro tier, ~21 months a customer, Saver persona
**Channel:** Zoom, recorded with consent

---

## Why this call

Priya was in Alex's interview log from February. She was articulate and opinionated and her renewal is in 9 weeks. I wanted my first own interview to be with someone Alex had already talked to — so I could compare what she says to me now vs what she told him three months ago.

---

## Transcript (abridged)

**Maya:** Thanks for jumping on, Priya. I joined two weeks ago, taking over from Alex. I read your conversation with him from February. I wanted to check in directly.

**Priya:** Sure. I'm honestly surprised someone followed up — Alex went a bit quiet after that call.

**Maya:** Back in February you told Alex the app was getting more cluttered and you wanted "a calculator with categories." Is that still how it feels?

**Priya:** Yes. More so. The streak counter is still there, the suggestions still get things wrong. I dismissed the goal widget the first day. I want less, not more.

**Maya:** When you say "less" — if I could change one thing for you, what would it be?

**Priya:** Honestly? Let me hide the streak counter. Or let me opt out of all the engagement stuff and just have the category view. A "minimal mode."

**Maya:** Got it. I want to ask about a couple of things that have been coming up in customer requests. Couples / shared budgets. You mentioned in February you keep finances separate with your partner. Is that still true?

**Priya:** Yeah. I will say — my sister, who is married, told me last month she's been using Monarch with her husband because they wanted shared categories. I didn't recommend Tally because, you know, you don't do that. But she'd have paid for it.

**Maya:** Interesting, thanks for naming that. Last thing — has anything changed in how you use the app since February?

**Priya:** I'm using it less. Maybe twice a week now. I used to be daily. It's the friction with the new stuff. Not enough to make me leave, but enough that I'm not as engaged.

## Maya's notes

- She is still a clean Saver signal. Wants less, not more. Confirms the "Saver wants minimal" pattern from Jordan's 2024 personas research — that pattern survived 20 months.
- "Minimal mode" / opt-out of engagement features is an idea that came up unprompted. Worth opening as a candidate hypothesis. Note that this is one signal from one person — don't promote.
- The sister-using-Monarch comment is a second-hand signal but lines up with the CS monthly's couples-budgeting trend. Tag it as low-strength corroborating evidence, not as a primary observation.
- She's using the app **less**. This matches the W4 retention drop in the April metrics snapshot. One data point doesn't confirm causation, but it's the kind of qualitative color the analytics number was missing.
- She did NOT mention spreadsheet export. (Counterpoint to the CS monthly's #2 ranking — at least one Saver doesn't share that ask.)
