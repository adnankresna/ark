**Interviewee:** Maya Okonkwo, Talent Operations Lead at LargeCo (~1,400 headcount, B2B logistics SaaS). On HireFlow for ~16 months.
**Interviewer:** Dana Liu (PM).
**Format:** 40-min Zoom, recorded with consent.

---

**Dana:** Tell me what your team does and what you do personally.

**Maya:** Talent Ops is four people. We're not recruiters and we're not hiring managers. We're the operational layer underneath both. I run the team. We own: interview scheduling end-to-end, interviewer training and panel composition, the analytics that go into the weekly hiring funnel review, vendor management for our sourcing tools, and the candidate experience — making sure candidates get the right comms at the right time.

**Dana:** That's broader than I expected. Sam at MidCo described a similar role but more scheduling-focused — does her version match a junior member of your team?

**Maya:** Yeah, our two Coordinators do roughly what she described. I do the higher-order stuff — interviewer training, panel composition strategy, vendor decisions. But the throughline is the same: we are the operational connective tissue. Recruiters source and screen. Hiring managers decide. We make the machine run.

**Dana:** How does HireFlow show up in your day?

**Maya:** I'm in the analytics tab a lot — pulling funnel data for the weekly review with the VP of Talent. I'm in the interviewer-management section maybe twice a week. The Coordinators on my team live in scheduling, which mostly means they live OUTSIDE HireFlow — in Google Calendar, in Slack, in Modern Loop. HireFlow holds the candidate record, but the workflow happens elsewhere.

**Dana:** What would a great HireFlow look like for the operational layer specifically?

**Maya:** Three things. (1) Real availability that interviewers actually maintain — right now we re-collect it every quarter and it's stale by week three. (2) Panel composition rules — "this req needs one staff-level interviewer, one cross-functional partner, one hiring manager delegate" — that HireFlow enforces when the scheduling kicks off. (3) Real analytics on interviewer load. Right now I cannot answer "which interviewers are doing more than 4 hours of interviewing per week" without a spreadsheet export.

**Dana:** When you decide what to buy, who do you talk to?

**Maya:** I evaluate. I make the recommendation. The VP of Talent signs off. Finance approves above $50k/year. Recruiters and hiring managers get a heads-up but they don't gate the decision.

**Dana:** Would your team pay more for HireFlow if it covered the operational layer?

**Maya:** If it replaced Modern Loop, yes. Modern Loop is $32k/year for us. If HireFlow did 80% of what Modern Loop does, with the candidate record already integrated, that's a clear win.

**Dana:** Last one: how distinct does your role feel from "recruiter" and "hiring manager"? Could a recruiter do your job in a pinch?

**Maya:** Different skill set. Recruiters are good at relationships and pitch. We're good at process design and systems. We've tried having recruiters cover when a Coordinator is out — it doesn't work, the recruiter resents it and the candidates feel it. Different job, different person.
